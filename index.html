The Problem

Adoption is not deployment.

Most companies have purchased AI. Very few have AI that reliably carries operational work — every day, with appropriate controls.

  • Idle licenses. Tools acquired over the past two years that were never integrated into actual workflows.
  • Prototype-grade automation. Workflows that performed well in a demonstration and degrade quietly within weeks, unnoticed.
  • Absent governance. AI is either permitted to touch nothing or trusted with everything. Neither posture survives contact with real operations.
  • No accountable owner. AI initiatives sit with whoever has spare capacity, rather than with an operator responsible for outcomes.

Operating Principles

Reliability is the product.

The market is saturated with demonstration-grade automation. The discipline below is what keeps systems in service at month three — and it is the standard every engagement is built to.

01

Human-in-the-loop by design

AI carries the repetitive majority of the work; your team retains the judgment calls. Writes to systems of record require approval.

02

Full transparency and ownership

Systems are built on your accounts and infrastructure, fully documented. You retain complete ownership and operational continuity, independent of me.

03

Self-reporting systems

Every workflow carries health monitoring and alerting. Failures are surfaced immediately rather than discovered downstream.

04

Data governance by routing

Sensitive material can be processed on locally hosted models; only approved data reaches cloud providers. Spend is capped per workflow.

05

Resident oversight agent

Each engagement includes a dedicated monitoring agent on your infrastructure — observing system health, resolving routine issues autonomously, and escalating directly to me through a shared channel.
